"15 The Lord God took the man and put him in the Garden of Eden to work it and take care of it. 16 And the Lord God commanded the man, “You are free to eat from any tree in the garden; 17 but you must not eat from the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, for when you eat from it you will certainly die.”

~Genesis: 1: 15-17



As far as we know, the above passage is the first time the Garden of Eden is mentioned in human history. Religious or not, we can all mostly agree on that.


The premise of the Garden is this; it is on earth, plentiful and abundant in food, greenery, life and essentially has anything man could ever need within in boundaries. Oh and at its center is the tree of knowledge of good and evil, which man should not eat from.


Historically speaking, we can understand that whoever wrote this believed humans originated from a single location.


It was upon eating from the tree of knowledge at this single location, analogous to obtaining knowledge and/or consciousness, that man became aware he could die.


From there, humanity would go on to engage in more evil deeds, straying further and further from its duty to take care for Eden as we attempted to evade death out of fear. Also known as the root of all evil.


Biblically speaking, from the moment Adam and Eve ate from the tree of knowledge, disobeying God in the process, humanity became sinful, was cast out, and determined to no longer be worthy of the Garden of Eden.


I whole heartedly disagree with this.


In fact, while I respect the Bible and have gained wisdom from its teachings, I think we’re uniquely capable, especially modern humans, of creating the Garden of Eden yet again.


We may have knowledge of good and evil, which may result in us acting evil or sinful, but it also means we can choose to act on what we believe to be good.


The Garden of Eden does not necessarily have to be a single Garden in-between four rivers with a magic tree at its center.


The Garden of Eden can be Earth itself and humanity can once again devout ourselves to taking care of it. We can choose to enhance our knowledge of the natural world, to heal forests, deserts, ocean reefs, and near extinct populations.


At our center, spiritually more-so rather than physically, knowledge and reason (i.e. the tree) can connect us. The fruits of knowledge we’ve gained in our pursuits of sciences, art, history, and many other forms can inform our decisions for the long term health of our planet its inhabitants.


We can continue developing our technology and creating more advanced clean energy and sustainable solutions. Until one day; we create a lush, vibrant, and abundant earth.


An earth so sustainable that each day, rather than gradually turn to desert, it becomes greener and even more diverse in life. This would be what I call like to call Modern Day Eden.
